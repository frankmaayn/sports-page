23 Feb 2020, 9:12 | Updated: 23 Feb 2020, 9:19

TYSON FURY obliterated Deontay Wilder to be crowned the new WBC heavyweight champ - and the punch stats emphasise the dominance. The Gypsy King floored Wilder twice during a stunning performance at the MGM Grand in Las Vegas before the towel was thrown in by the American's corner. Fury won EVERY ROUND on two of the judges' scorecards, and the fights stats from Compubox tell the story. Fury threw more than double the amount of punches as Wilder. Over the six-and-a-bit rounds the newly-crowned heavyweight king landed 82 of 267 punches thrown, a 30.7 per cent strike rate. Of these 24 were jabs and 58 were power punches. Wilder only landed 34 of 141 - a 24.1 per cent effort. Sixteen were jabs and 18 were power punches. The fact that someone described as the most powerful puncher in boxing landed less than a third of the power punches Fury had success with shows just how the fight went. The 31-year-old Morecambe man landed these 58 power punches in less than seven rounds, whereas he had success with just 38 in their first encounter.
